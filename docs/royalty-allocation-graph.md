# Royalty Allocation Graph

## 1. Overview

**Royalty Allocation Graph** is the v0.4 layer of Origin Structure Market.

v0.1 defines how to register an upstream structure as an **Origin Asset**.
v0.2 defines how to register a downstream **Derivative Asset**.
v0.3 defines how to evaluate derivative claims through an **Origin Audit Record**.
v0.4 defines how to convert revenue events, audited lineage, contribution scores, and allocation policies into a proposed royalty distribution.

In short:

```text
Origin Asset
    ↓
Derivative Asset
    ↓
Origin Audit Record
    ↓
Royalty Allocation Graph
```

The Royalty Allocation Graph provides a machine-readable allocation record for distributing revenue across:

* final creators
* origin creators
* derivative creators
* auditors
* protocol funds
* marketplace operators
* commons funds
* other relevant participants

This layer does not execute payment by itself.
It defines the allocation graph that settlement systems, smart contracts, marketplace logic, or human reviewers may later use.

---

## 2. Purpose

Origin Audit Record asks:

> How credible is the derivative claim, and what evidence supports it?

Royalty Allocation Graph asks:

> Given a revenue event and audited lineage, how should value be distributed?

The purpose of v0.4 is to create a structured bridge between:

```text
revenue event → audit evidence → contribution scores → allocation graph
```

This makes value flow visible before payment execution.

---

## 3. Design Principles

### 3.1 Allocation before settlement

A payment system should not execute before an allocation structure exists.

The Royalty Allocation Graph records the proposed distribution first.
Settlement can then happen later through off-chain payment, marketplace accounting, smart contracts, or manual review.

### 3.2 Audit-based distribution

Allocation should not rely only on self-declared claims.

The graph may reference Origin Audit Records and contribution scores to justify why value flows to specific recipients.

### 3.3 Human review boundary

Because royalty allocation can affect real compensation, human review remains important.

A graph may be:

* draft
* proposed
* pending review
* approved
* partially paid
* paid
* disputed
* cancelled

This prevents automatic payment from being triggered by unreviewed or disputed data.

### 3.4 Payment-neutral design

v0.4 does not require blockchain.

It supports:

* off-chain settlement
* on-chain settlement
* hybrid settlement
* manual settlement
* future marketplace settlement

This keeps the protocol usable before full smart contract integration.

## 5. Required Fields

A valid Royalty Allocation Graph must include:

```text
royalty_allocation_graph:
  id
  revenue_event_id
  product_id
  timestamp
  currency
  gross_revenue
  allocation_basis
  allocations
  settlement
  human_review
  trace
```

These fields define the revenue event, product, allocation logic, recipient entries, settlement state, review boundary, and trace evidence.

---

## 6. Field Structure

### 6.1 id

Unique identifier for the allocation graph.

Example:

```yaml
id: allocation-2026-0001
```

The identifier must follow this pattern:

```text
allocation-YYYY-NNNN
```

---

### 6.2 revenue_event_id

Identifier of the revenue event that triggered the allocation graph.

Example:

```yaml
revenue_event_id: revenue-2026-0001
```

The identifier must follow this pattern:

```text
revenue-YYYY-NNNN
```

---

### 6.3 product_id

Identifier of the product, listing, derivative asset, or final output that generated revenue.

Example:

```yaml
product_id: derivative-2026-0001
```

Supported ID prefixes include:

```text
product
listing
derivative
```

---

### 6.4 product_title

Optional human-readable title of the revenue-generating product.

Example:

```yaml
product_title: Origin Asset Generator GPT
```

---

### 6.5 currency

Currency used for the revenue event and allocation graph.

Example:

```yaml
currency: JPY
```

The value should use a three-letter currency code such as:

```text
JPY
USD
EUR
```

---

### 6.6 gross_revenue

Gross revenue generated by the product or revenue event.

Example:

```yaml
gross_revenue: 10000
```

This is the total revenue before optional deductions.

---

### 6.7 net_revenue

Optional net revenue after platform fees, taxes, refunds, or other deductions.

Example:

```yaml
net_revenue: 10000
```

If deductions are not yet modeled, `net_revenue` may match `gross_revenue`.

---

## 7. Revenue Source

The optional `revenue_source` section records where the revenue came from.

Example:

```yaml
revenue_source:
  channel: gpt_store
  platform: GPT Store
  transaction_reference: gpt-store-revenue-2026-0001
```

Supported channels include:

```text
marketplace
gpt_store
github_sponsors
blog
bookstore
saas
license
subscription
direct_sale
hybrid
other
```

This section helps connect the allocation graph to real-world commercial events.

---

## 8. Allocation Basis

The `allocation_basis` section defines why the allocation is calculated the way it is.

Example:

```yaml
allocation_basis:
  basis_type: audit_based
  audit_record_ids:
    - audit-2026-0001
  origin_asset_ids:
    - origin-2026-0001
  derivative_asset_ids:
    - derivative-2026-0001
  contribution_scores:
    origin-2026-0001: 0.45
    derivative-2026-0001: 0.55
  policy:
    name: Kazene-Origin-Royalty-Policy-0.4
    allocation_mode: audit_weighted
```

The allocation basis may include:

* audit record references
* origin asset references
* derivative asset references
* contribution scores
* allocation policy

---

## 9. Basis Type

The `basis_type` field records the primary basis used for allocation.

Supported values include:

```text
declared_split
audit_based
contract_based
marketplace_policy
hybrid
manual_review
other
```

Example:

```yaml
basis_type: audit_based
```

This means the allocation is based primarily on audit-supported lineage and contribution scores.

---

## 10. Contribution Scores

The `contribution_scores` field records contribution weights used as allocation inputs.

Example:

```yaml
contribution_scores:
  origin-2026-0001: 0.45
  derivative-2026-0001: 0.55
```

These values may come from:

* Derivative Asset contribution claims
* Origin Audit Record contribution scores
* human review
* marketplace policy
* hybrid evaluation

Important:

> Contribution scores are inputs to allocation, not automatic payments by themselves.

---

## 11. Allocation Policy

The `policy` section records the policy used to convert contribution evidence into allocation rates.

Example:

```yaml
policy:
  name: Kazene-Origin-Royalty-Policy-0.4
  allocation_mode: audit_weighted
  description: >
    This allocation policy converts audit-supported lineage, contribution
    scores, derivative participation, and protocol maintenance requirements
    into a proposed royalty allocation graph.
```

Supported allocation modes include:

```text
fixed_rate
contribution_weighted
audit_weighted
contract_defined
manual
hybrid
```

This field allows future systems to distinguish between fixed splits, audit-based splits, manual splits, and contract-defined splits.

---

## 12. Allocations

The `allocations` section is the core of v0.4.

It records how revenue is distributed across recipients.

Example:

```yaml
allocations:
  - recipient_id: final-creator-samuraiwriter7
    recipient_role: final_creator
    related_asset_id: derivative-2026-0001
    rate: 0.85
    amount: 8500
    currency: JPY
    settlement_status: proposed
```

Each allocation entry includes:

* recipient ID
* recipient role
* related asset ID
* rate
* amount
* currency
* calculation note
* settlement status

---

## 13. Recipient Roles

Supported recipient roles include:

```text
final_creator
origin_creator
derivative_creator
auditor
protocol_fund
marketplace_operator
license_holder
commons_fund
tax_reserve
other
```

Example:

```yaml
recipient_role: origin_creator
```

This allows the graph to distinguish between the final product creator and upstream contributors.

---

## 14. Allocation Rate and Amount

Each allocation entry includes both `rate` and `amount`.

Example:

```yaml
rate: 0.05
amount: 500
currency: JPY
```

In this case:

```text
gross_revenue: 10000 JPY
rate: 0.05
amount: 500 JPY
```

The protocol records the result explicitly so downstream systems do not need to recalculate the amount blindly.

---

## 15. Settlement Status

Each allocation entry has its own settlement status.

Supported values include:

```text
proposed
pending
approved
paid
withheld
disputed
cancelled
```

Example:

```yaml
settlement_status: proposed
```

This makes it possible to track partial payment, disputes, or withheld allocations.

---

## 16. Settlement

The `settlement` section records the overall settlement mechanism and status.

Example:

```yaml
settlement:
  mechanism: hybrid
  status: proposed
  payment_reference: royalty-allocation-proposal-2026-0001
  notes: >
    This allocation graph is a proposed settlement record.
```

Supported mechanisms include:

```text
off_chain
on_chain
hybrid
manual
unspecified
```

Supported settlement statuses include:

```text
draft
proposed
pending_review
approved
partially_paid
paid
disputed
cancelled
```

This separates allocation design from payment execution.

---

## 17. Human Review Boundary

The `human_review` section prevents proposed allocations from becoming binding automatically.

Example:

```yaml
human_review:
  required: true
  status: pending
  reviewer: protocol-maintainer
  notes: >
    Human review is required before this allocation graph becomes binding.
```

Supported review statuses include:

```text
not_required
pending
approved
rejected
disputed
```

This boundary is especially important when:

* real compensation is involved
* contribution scores are disputed
* smart contract settlement is planned
* marketplace payouts depend on the graph
* tax, legal, or contractual obligations may apply

## 21. Relationship to Future Layers

Royalty Allocation Graph prepares the protocol for marketplace integration.

```text
v0.1 Origin Asset Registration
    ↓
v0.2 Derivative Asset Registration
    ↓
v0.3 Origin Audit Record
    ↓
v0.4 Royalty Allocation Graph
    ↓
v0.5 Marketplace Layer
```

v0.4 does not define public listing metadata yet.

It creates the allocation structure that v0.5 marketplace records may reference when assets are listed, licensed, sold, or reused.

---

## 22. Summary

Royalty Allocation Graph upgrades Origin Structure Market from an auditable lineage protocol into a proposed economic allocation protocol.

v0.1 asks:

> What is the origin structure?

v0.2 asks:

> What was derived from it, how, and with what claimed contribution?

v0.3 asks:

> How credible is the derivative claim, and what evidence supports it?

v0.4 asks:

> How should revenue be allocated across creators, origins, derivatives, auditors, and protocol funds?

This is the layer where traceable lineage begins to become economic circulation.

It does not move money by itself.
It defines the map through which money can later move.
